Life of Blue Morpho

As its common name implies, the blue morpho butterfly’s wings are bright blue, edged with black. The blue morpho is among the largest butterflies in the world, with wings spanning from five to eight inches. Their vivid, iridescent blue coloring is a result of the microscopic scales on the backs of their wings, which reflect light. The underside of the morpho’s wings, on the other hand, is a dull brown color with many eyespots, providing camouflage against predators such as birds and insects when its wings are closed. When the blue morpho flies, the contrasting bright blue and dull brown colors flash, making it look like the morpho is appearing and disappearing. The males’ wings are broader than those of the females and appear to be brighter in color. Blue morphos, like other butterflies, also have two clubbed antennas, two fore wings and two hind wings, six legs and three body segments—the head, thorax, and abdomen. Native to the rainforests of Mexico, Central America and South America, this bright blue butterfly has a short but stunning life.

Facts about Blue Morpho

  • Habitat: Blue morphos live in the tropical forests of Latin America from Mexico to Colombia.
  • Diet: The blue morpho’s diet changes throughout each stage of its lifecycle.
  • Threats: Blue morphos are threatened by deforestation of tropical forests and habitat fragmentation.
  • Colour: Created by the physical interactions of light with tiny structures, is known as structural colour.
